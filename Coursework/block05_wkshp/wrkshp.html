Zoo Animal Directory

Bears

A bear is a large, strong mammal with a thick fur coat. Bears are known for their powerful build, sharp claws, and keen sense of smell. They typically live in forests and mountains, and are omnivorous, meaning they eat both plants and animals. Bears are often recognized by their iconic hump on their back and are found in various species, such as grizzly bears, polar bears, and black bears. More Information

Giraffes

A giraffe is a tall, long-necked mammal native to Africa. It is known for its unique spotted coat, long neck, and distinctive ossicones (horn-like structures) on its head. Giraffes are the tallest land animals, with legs and necks that can reach up to 18 feet in length. They mainly feed on leaves and twigs from trees using their long necks, and their spotted pattern helps them blend into their natural habitat. More Information

  • Frankie
  • Coconut

Lions

A lion is a large, strong cat with golden fur and a big mane. They live in Africa and some parts of India. Lions live in groups called prides and are famous for their loud roar. They are excellent hunters, often chasing and catching big animals like zebras and buffalo. Lions are often called the "King of the Jungle" and are known for their courage and strength. Monkeys

A monkey is a small to medium-sized primate known for its playful and intelligent behavior. Monkeys have a furry body, a long tail, and a flexible, grasping hand. They live in trees in tropical regions and often swing from branch to branch. Monkeys are social animals that live in groups called troops and are known for their curiosity and ability to use tools. Alligators

An alligator is a large, reptilian animal that lives in freshwater habitats like swamps and rivers. It has a broad, flat body covered in tough, armored skin and a long, powerful tail. Alligators have a wide, rounded snout and sharp teeth, which they use for hunting fish, birds, and other small animals. They are excellent swimmers and can move quickly both in water and on land. Alligators are known for their distinctive bellowing sound and are often found in the southeastern United States and parts of China. More Information
